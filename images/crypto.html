Crypto is a legend that was released in Season 3. He can be unlocked using 750 or 12,000.

A Recon Legend, Crypto takes to the skies with his Surveillance Drone, revealing enemies for both Crypto and his teammates, thanks to Neurolink. And if he finds that the enemies have already hunkered down, he can activate his Drone EMP to break their defenses and slow them before he comes out of the shadows and makes his move with his squad.

Biography[edit | edit source]

Crypto specializes in secrets; he knows how to uncover them, and how to keep them. A brilliant hacker and encryption expert, he uses aerial drones to spy on his opponents in the Apex Arena without being seen.

Orphans raised on the streets of Suotamo, Tae Joon and his foster sister Mila Alexander escaped a life of squalor by becoming computer engineers, designing drones used by the Syndicate to transmit the Apex Games throughout the Outlands. But one day, Tae Joon and Mila accidentally discovered a prediction algorithm used by the Syndicate to rig bets for the Apex Games, leading to Mila’s mysterious murder - and the framing of Tae Joon for it. With his reputation destroyed, and the Syndicate after him, he gave up his identity, his face and his family in order to facilitate his redemption.

Wanting to get closer to the people who framed him, Tae Joon wanted to join the games but thought he wouldn't be able to pass the games' qualifiers. He did what he does best by hacking into the games' mainframe and putting himself in their records as a legend, but not before blowing up Kings Canyon's Repulsor Tower to serve as a distraction.[1] This allowed the entry of hostile wildlife on Kings Canyon and disruption of the games.

Now joining the games as the combatant known as Crypto, he seeks justice - and revenge.

Abilities[edit | edit source]

Surveillance Drone[edit | edit source]

Surveillance Drone
Type Tactical
Cooldown 40 seconds
Description Deploys an aerial drone. 40-second cooldown if destroyed.

  • The drone can be deployed and flown up to 200 meters away from Crypto. If the drone is 240+ meters away from Crypto, it'll automatically be recalled.
  • The drone can ping items as long as they are within 12 meters. Enemies can be pinged up to 125 meters.
  • When not being piloted manually, the drone can be parked in midair or recalled to Crypto. The recall takes 3 seconds.
  • The drone has 30 health, and only heals damage when recalled.
  • The drone can be used to open doors and supply bins and can pick up teammates' banner cards. It must be within 7 meters to do this.
  • Looking at a banner display with the drone lists the number of enemy squads within the 200-meter radius.
    • Crypto can warn teammates of nearby squads by pinging a banner.
  • Crypto is left stationary while piloting the drone. Taking damage while in drone view will force Crypto out of it.
  • Primary weapons automatically reload while in drone view.

  • The following is everything that the Surveillance Drone can Ping:
    • Gear
    • Weapons
    • Attachments
    • Consumables
    • Ammo
    • Grenades
    • Map Features
    • Enemies
  • Pathfinder's Grappling Hook can attach to the drone.
  • The drone can be stuck with a teammate's Arc Star.
  • The drone cannot pass through Wraith's Dimensional Rift.

  • Although your team can see everything, try to ping locations to help your team out.
  • Crypto is completely vulnerable while in drone view. Find an out of the way place to stay while managing the drone.
  • Sometimes you can use the Drone as a decoy so the enemy or enemies will be distracted to shoot it, while you're either flanking or escaping.
  • If your squadmates have died outside the ring and you're inside the ring, you can use Surveillance Drone to safely retrieve your squadmates' banner.
  • Crypto can also recall the Surveillance Drone while he is currently using a consumable item such as a Medkit or Shield Battery, as both only require one hand. This can allow you to reposition the drone after healing.
  • Holding the tactical button allows Crypto to deploy his drone without needing to enter the drone view.
  • Because the drone appears with an orange aura for enemies, using it outside ring edges blends the orange aura with the orange ring effect, making it harder for enemies to spot. They’ll still know there’s a drone nearby, however.

  • The Surveillance Drone can sometimes take more shots to destroy than intended despite only having 30 health. This doesn't include weapons that can one shot it such as the Wingman.

Neurolink[edit | edit source]

Neurolink
Type Passive
Cooldown N/A
Description Crypto and his teammates see what the Surveillance Drone detects up to a 30-meter radius.

  • The drone will highlight nearby doors, supply bins, traps, and enemies in its line of sight. Enemies are more noticeably marked with a triangle.
  • Enemies seen by the drone will see 'DRONE DETECTED' on their HUD.

  • Mirage cannot be detected while cloaked from Now You See Me....
  • Mirages decoys can be scanned but won't be highlighted as an enemy.
  • Enemies won't be highlighted if Gibraltar's Dome of Protection comes between them and the drone.
  • If Crypto is inside enemy or friendly Bangalore smoke while in recon mode your screen will start to become fuzzy.
  • Enemies cannot be detected through Bangalore’s smoke or Caustic’s gas.

  • The Drone EMP and Neurolink can be used even when Crypto is not directly controlling the drone. This actively allows Crypto to stay in the fight or gather information on what the enemy/enemies are doing.
  • If you're struggling to look for the last few squads. Using the drone to fly around in a 30-meter radius while opening doors and going through buildings etc., you might have a chance of the enemy/enemies being marked by your drone as you flew by them without even realizing they were hiding in a corner. Giving your squad the information you need.

Drone EMP[edit | edit source]

Drone EMP
Type Ultimate
Charge time 180 seconds
Description Charge up an EMP blast from your drone. Deals 50 shield damage, slow players, and disables traps.

  • Takes 3 seconds to charge before detonating.
  • Affects all enemy players (and Crypto himself) in its 30-meter radius, even through walls and cover. Teammates are unaffected.
  • The slow lasts roughly 1-2 seconds.

  • The following is everything that the Drone EMP will destroy:
    • Gibraltar's Dome of Protection
    • Lifeline's D.O.C. Heal Drone
    • Wattson's Perimeter Fences
    • Caustic's Nox Gas Trap
    • Enemy Crypto's Surveillance Drone
  • The following is everything that will take 50 damage from Drone EMP:
    • x22px Body Armor or Evo Shield that players dropped.
  • EMP will deal 100 damage instead of 50 to the following:
    • Wattson's Interception Pylon
    • Octane's Launch Pad

  • The radius of the blast is the same as Neurolink's tracking radius. If an enemy is highlighted in orange, you know they'll be caught in the blast.
  • You can activate EMP even when you are not in the drone view. The EMP will be activated where you last leave the drone. This way, you can set the drone in one spot, leave the drone view, prepare for an ambush, activate the EMP, and rush immediately.
  • Keep in mind that you and your drone are still vulnerable to damage while EMP is charging.
  • Be mindful that your drone is easier to spot while EMP is charging. Try to activate EMP in a tough to reach/aim spot to keep your drone safe.
  • Using the EMP on enemies who are fighting or escaping outside The Ring can be effective as the slow effect can potentially be enough for the ring to knock or eliminate them.
  • Using EMP on single targets is well worth doing. This can take an enemy out of the team fight for a few seconds if you solo EMP them rather than their team to group up. Solo EMPs also have more chances of being successful than it getting shot down.
  • EMP is very good for ending team fights quickly to and lessening the chances of your team getting third partied.
  • As a Crypto main, it's best to carry at least 1-2 Ultimate Accelerant in your inventory to quickly build another EMP.
  • When paired with Wraith, having her create a Dimensional Rift as you follow her while manually piloting the Drone to EMP. Take the portal to force a team fight as well as having an escape route back to safety just encase the team fight is failing.

Trivia[edit | edit source]

  • Crypto was teased for an entire season before his official reveal:
    • Crypto's first appearance was in the trailer for Season 2, where he was responsible for destroying the Repulsor tower.
    • Crypto was the source of several 'glitches' in Kings Canyon, including distorted lines from the announcer and hacked banner displays showing his attempts to get login information.
    • Crypto appeared in an inaccessible room behind glass in Singh Labs, getting info on the space elevator at World's Edge. When a player got near, he and his drone would flee.
  • Crypto shows his opinions about the other Legends very clearly in coded messages/letters to Mystik, his former caretaker:
    • He shows a strong disliking for Mirage, despite acknowledging Mystik's adoration for him. He finds Mirage too cocky and an idiot.
    • He assumes Caustic is nothing more than a sociopath and seems to avoid him out of intimidation.
    • He thought nothing of Gibraltar, but upon closer inspection, found him to be one of the few with morals.
  • Crypto's logo appears in "Voidwalker" when an alternate Wraith hacks into an abandoned IMC base.
  • As explained by writer Tom Casiello, the device around his jawline/ear alters his face, so he doesn’t look like himself from the past.[1]
  • He also said that the Hype Beast skin is "his innermost desires come to life. He wants to scream his identity to all within earshot. Because that would mean this nightmare is over."[2]
